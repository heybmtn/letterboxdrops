Good question

Does leafleting actually work?

Yes. And the reason why might surprise you.

01

The letterbox is the least competitive place in marketing

Everyone else is fighting over the same Facebook audiences and Google clicks. The letterbox? Barely any competition. Your leaflet doesn't compete with ten other ads — it arrives on its own.

02

People trust something they can hold

A leaflet on the kitchen counter says: this is a real local business. It builds credibility in a way a banner ad never could. In a world full of faceless digital noise, physical stands out.

03

It makes your other marketing work harder

Someone sees your leaflet on Monday, searches your name on Thursday, calls on Friday. The leaflet started that chain. It's not luck — it's how local businesses consistently win new customers.

04

Your competitors have already given up on it

Most local businesses abandoned leafleting years ago. That's left the letterbox wide open. If your rivals aren't showing up in people's homes, you can own that space entirely.

The businesses winning locally right now aren't choosing between digital and physical. They're doing both. A well-targeted leaflet drop is one of the most cost-effective things a local business can do.
