Good question

Is leafleting still worth it?

Yes. And here's the bit most people miss.

01

The letterbox is the least crowded place in marketing

Everybody's fighting online. Inboxes are rammed. Social feeds are exhausting. Digital ads get scrolled past before they've even registered. But the letterbox? Quiet. Calm. Barely any competition. Your leaflet lands there and it can't be swiped away.

02

Physical beats digital for trust. Every time.

A leaflet on the kitchen counter says: we're a real business. We invest in this area. We're not some faceless ad that disappeared the moment you scrolled past. In a world drowning in AI-generated content, something physical builds trust fast.

03

It makes everything else work harder

Leaflets don't replace digital. They supercharge it. Someone sees your leaflet Monday, finds your Facebook page Thursday, calls you Friday. The leaflet started that journey. That's not a coincidence. That's how local businesses win.

04

Your competitors have stopped. You shouldn't.

Most local businesses abandoned leafleting for digital. That's left the letterbox wide open. Less competition. More attention. If your rivals aren't showing up there, you could have that space entirely to yourself.

The businesses winning locally in 2026 aren't choosing between digital and physical. They're doing both. A well-targeted leaflet drop is one of the highest-ROI moves a local business can make right now.
