Good question

Is leafleting still worth it?

Short answer: yes. Here's why.

Your customers' letterboxes aren't crowded

Everyone's fighting for attention online. Inboxes are full, social feeds are noisy, and digital ads get scrolled past in a second. But the letterbox? That's quiet. A physical leaflet lands in a space where there's almost no competition — and it can't be ignored with a swipe.

People trust something they can hold

A leaflet feels real. It shows you're an actual business, invested in your local area. In a world of faceless online ads and AI-generated content, something physical on the kitchen counter builds credibility in a way a Facebook ad simply can't.

It works alongside your other marketing

Leaflets don't replace digital — they strengthen it. A homeowner sees your leaflet on Monday, spots your Facebook page on Thursday, and calls you on Friday. The leaflet started the conversation. That's how local businesses win.

Your competitors probably aren't doing it

Most small businesses have moved entirely online, which means leaflet distribution is a quieter, less competitive space than it's been in years. If your competitors aren't in the letterbox, you could be the only one there.

The businesses winning locally in 2026 are the ones combining digital with physical. A well-targeted leaflet drop is one of the most cost-effective tools a small business has.
